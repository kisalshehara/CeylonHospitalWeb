FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Explore our FAQ section to learn about Ceylon Hospitals, Sri Lanka's premier private hospital specializing in all indigenous medical systems. Find answers to common questions about our services, and the exceptional care provided by our team of over a thousand registered indigenous medical officers.
Contact now Contact now
  • What is Ceylon Hospitals ?
    Ceylon Hospitals is a pioneering healthcare institution in Sri Lanka, renowned for being the first and only private hospital offering comprehensive services in all indigenous medical systems. We are a part of the Alpha Management Group (Pvt) Ltd., a reputed entity in the international business world.
  • What indigenous medical systems are available at Ceylon Hospitals?
    We offer treatments across all Sri Lankan indigenous medical systems, including Siddha, Unani, Ayurveda, and Traditional medicine. These systems have been practiced for thousands of years and are known for their unique therapeutic methods.
  • Can foreign patients receive treatment at Ceylon Hospitals?
    Yes, foreign patients are welcome at Ceylon Hospitals. We provide high-quality medical services, along with accommodation and other facilities to ensure a comfortable stay for our international patients. We also offer supportive services like language translation to assist our foreign guests.
  • What makes Ceylon Hospitals unique?
    Ceylon Hospitals is unique for its exclusive focus on Sri Lankan indigenous medicine. We are the only hospital that integrates all indigenous medical systems under one roof and brings this rich heritage to the global stage. Additionally, our collaboration with lecturers from Sri Lankan universities of indigenous medicine and traditional physicians enhances the quality and authenticity of our services.
  • How do I make an appointment at Ceylon Hospitals?
    You can make an appointment by contacting our hospital directly through our website or customer service hotline. We will guide you through the process and help you schedule a consultation with the appropriate medical officer.
